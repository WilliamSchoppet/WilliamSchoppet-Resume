I can't remember the specific moment that I told myself that I was going to be a history major, because I feel like I have always known. I grew up fascinated by history. Anything from movies, to videogames, to stories, to picture books, I loved all of it. History became the highlight of all of my academic filled years in school. When I think back on my time in highschool, middle school, and even elementary school, history is the subject I remember the most fondly.

As mentioned, my love for history grew out of my hobbies as a child and teenager. Through these hobbies I met people just like me who adored the same concepts that I did. This furthered my love of the subject, however my teachers were truly the biggest inspiration of all. There is something truly special about a highly educated and diligent person, who is knowledgeable about their interest, telling you all that they know. That is why when it came to the point I needed to apply for colleges, marking down history as my major was the easiest decision of my life.

As I am a current student enrolled full time at university, I have not had the time to gather much experience in my field. From what I know about the practice of history, it takes someone who is able to research diligently and to stay transfixed on their topic. As a student I have already gained this ability. Whether it be from writing my highschool senior thesis about Napoleon (my favorite historical figure), or a historical inquiry about the use of cannons at the siege of Constantnople in 1453, I have at least some experience in this matter. All I need to do now is hone my skills into something more.
